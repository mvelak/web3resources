## Information to include about the resources

For each resource you select, provide identifying information—for example,
title of the resource and the URL—in a consistent fashion. Then, describe the
resource in an abstract so that readers will understand its function and
scope. Be sure to make the abstract relevant to the task at hand and to your
specific audience and purpose. In other words, do not write generic
abstracts for anyone who might use the resource for anything.

In writing the abstracts, assume readers will ask the following questions:
 What is this?
 What is in it (content, scope)?
 How is the information relevant to and useful for my specific audience
and purpose?
 What tips can help someone use the resource efficiently and
effectively?

Be sure to provide a fully developed answer to each of these questions. Use
examples to help you elaborate the resources for your audience. A trap in
this assignment is writing superficial abstracts. Be patient as you describe
the resources.

## Evaluation criteria

I will evaluate the guides according to these expectations:

**Content** : The guide is complete, including all necessary information, and the
sources are significant and current (unless they serve a historical purpose).

**Organization and Design** : Readers will be able to find the information they
need. Related materials are grouped. The guide is “usable” according to the
issues we’ve discussed this term. The guide employs technical
communication conventions for the web. On both a macro (site) and micro
(page) level, the design is discernible and appropriate.

**Style** : Abstracts give specific information in efficient sentences (don’t
confuse efficiency and brevity). Abstracts have good topic sentences. The


style is deductive, with advance organizers (forecasting statements)
introducing information.

**Audience Adaptation** : The guide offers explanatory material or instructions
to help the audience achieve their purpose. Abstracts are fully elaborated
with the audience and purpose in mind.

**Mechanics** : Spelling, grammar, and punctuation are correct.
