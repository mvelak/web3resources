web3

/wɛb θriː/ (noun)

The third generation of the World Wide Web, characterized by decentralized protocols, blockchain technology, and user ownership of data and assets. Unlike its predecessors, Web3 aims to remove central intermediaries, enabling peer-to-peer interactions in a trustless environment.

Guide overview

Who the guide is for

This guide is designed for technical specialists, researchers, and developers who are transitioning from traditional web2 environments to the decentralized web3 landscape. It focuses on high-quality, specialist-to-specialist communication resources that provide deep technical insight rather than general consumer overviews.

Purpose of the guide

The primary purpose is to provide a curated path through the "noise" of the crypto space. By identifying six foundational resources (ranging from news and employment to peer-reviewed research and development tools), this guide empowers specialists to build, audit, and analyze decentralized applications effectively.

User assumptions

We assume users have a baseline understanding of computer science fundamentals, cryptographic principles (like public-private key pairs), and distributed systems. While expertise in solidity or rust is not required, familiarity with command-line interfaces and general software development lifecycles is recommended.

Organization of resources

The guide is organized into three functional clusters: market intelligence (The Block, Web3.Career), technical foundations (arXiv, Etherscan), and practical application (Remix IDE, OpenSea). This grouping allows specialists to prioritize resources based on their current project phase.

Tips for using the guide

To get the most out of this resource, we recommend starting with the spotlight to align on terminology, then following the functional clusters relevant to your role. Each detailed resource page includes quick navigation links to return to this overview at any time.
