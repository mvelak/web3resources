arXiv

Open-access archive for scholarly articles in technical fields.

What it is

arXiv is a free distribution service and an open-access archive for nearly 2.4 million scholarly articles in the fields of physics, mathematics, computer science, quantitative biology, quantitative finance, statistics, electrical engineering, and economics. Materials on the site are not peer-reviewed by arXiv. It is hosted at Cornell University and has functioned as the primary preprint repository in technical disciplines since 1991.

What's in it

For web3 and blockchain researchers, arXiv's computer science (cs) and economics (econ) sections are the key destinations. The cs.CR (cryptography and security) and cs.DC (distributed, parallel, and cluster computing) categories host foundational and cutting-edge work on zero-knowledge proofs, consensus mechanisms, layer-2 scaling, and smart contract security.

Relevance for Web3

arXiv is where the theoretical underpinnings of Web3 technologies are first published. The original Bitcoin whitepaper, foundational ZK-SNARK papers, Ethereum EIP research, and attack analyses of major protocols have all appeared on arXiv before or alongside publication. It serves as a critical resource for specialists who need to understand the mathematical and structural integrity of the protocols they build upon.

Tips for Efficient Use

Use arXiv's advanced search at arxiv.org/search/advanced to filter by category (e.g., cs.CR), date range, and keyword. We recommend subscribing to the RSS feeds for specific categories to stay updated on the latest preprints without manual searching.
